The Greeting Cards

Most of us have received or sent a greeting card, keeping some as treasured mementos. But have you ever wondered about the greeting cards collected by design museums like the Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian?
This project explores their collection of 577 greeting cards, uncovering the design elements that make these cards memorable.

We usually send greeting cards for specific occasions, and some occasions are more popular than others. Most of the cards in this collection were donated to the museum by people like you. Let’s explore the most popular occasions.

Each occasion features a variety of design elements and colors that represent it. If we examine the vibrant colors of these cards, some palettes stand out as more popular than others. Warm tones dominate the collection, while blue tones are less common.

When it comes to design elements, they are less varied than colors. Many designs repeat similar themes, which can be grouped into 10 categories. Most cards feature straightforward, easy-to-understand visuals rather than abstract designs, and many do not include text on the cover, relying solely on imagery.

The combination of colors and elements for each occasion also varies slightly. Nature-themed cards are not predominantly green, while geometric designs are often the most vibrant and colorful

Methodology

The data for this project comes from the Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum API. The cards were categorized manually based on their titles, description texts, or the text that appears on the cards. Colors were analyzed using Vibrant.js, so some results may not be entirely accurate.
